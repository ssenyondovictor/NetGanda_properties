California Online Privacy Protection Act (CalOPPA)

CalOPPA requires businesses that collect personal information from California residents to post a privacy policy on their website. The privacy policy must state what personal information is collected, how it is used, and with whom it is shared.

We comply with CalOPPA by:

  • Posting this Privacy Policy on our website
  • Not collecting any personal information from California residents
  • Not sharing any personal information with third parties

Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA)

COPPA protects the privacy of children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect or use personal information from children under the age of 13. If you are under the age of 13, please do not use our website or services.

Fair Information Practices

The Fair Information Practices Principles form the backbone of privacy law in the United States. These principles require businesses to:

  • Notify individuals about the collection and use of their personal information
  • Give individuals the opportunity to opt out of the collection and use of their personal information
  • Take reasonable steps to protect personal information from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, alteration, or destruction

We comply with the Fair Information Practices Principles by:

  • Posting this Privacy Policy on our website
  • Giving you the opportunity to opt out of the collection and use of your personal information
  • Taking reasonable steps to protect your personal information

CAN-SPAM Act

The CAN-SPAM Act regulates the sending of commercial email. We comply with the CAN-SPAM Act by:

  • Not sending unsolicited commercial email
  • Providing a clear and concise unsubscribe link in all commercial email
  • Honoring unsubscribe requests within 10 business days
